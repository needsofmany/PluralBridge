Your export may be the bridge.

A Simply Plural export may now be the most complete copy of your System’s data. Keep it private, keep a backup, and keep it somewhere you control.

Please do not send us Simply Plural tokens, passwords, export files, screenshots, or private System data.

What an export may contain

A Simply Plural export can contain sensitive System data even when the files look ordinary.

  • Member records and profile data.
  • Notes and text records where available.
  • Avatar images where available.
  • Fronting history and timestamps.
  • Custom fields, privacy buckets, groups, and related exported data.

What to do with your export now

  • Keep the export in a private folder or drive you control.
  • Make at least one backup copy somewhere safe.
  • Keep the export out of public GitHub repositories, paste sites, screenshots, and public support threads.
  • Do not email or direct-message the export to PluralBridge or anyone else unless you have made a deliberate private-data decision.
  • Remember where the export is stored so you can use it when the PluralBridge import path is ready.

What changed after shutdown

The next useful step is import preview: a way to inspect preserved Simply Plural data before anything is saved into PluralBridge.
